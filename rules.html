NOESIS 2K25-26

Eligibility

  • NOESIS 2K25-26 is open to undergraduate students enrolled in BE, BCA, BSc (Computer Science/IT), B.Voc, and B.Tech programs, both within and outside the state of Goa.
  • Teams from outside Goa must be accompanied by a faculty member from their institution.

Registration & Fees

Team Composition

  • Only one team per college is allowed.
  • Each team must provide a letter of authorization signed by the Principal of their college.
  • All team members must carry valid college ID cards.
  • A team should consist of 10 students: 8 for main events and 2 for gaming

Event Participation

  • The competition features five categories of events, each requiring two students.
  • Students selecting a category must participate in all sub-events under that category.
  • Once a category is chosen, participants cannot switch to a different category.
  • Events under the “Swing of Fortune” category are compulsory and included for grading.

General Instruction

Code of Conduct

  • Participants are expected to maintain professionalism and respectful behavior throughout the event. Misconduct may lead to team disqualification.

Punctuality

  • Teams must strictly adhere to the event schedule.

Dress Code

  • Participants should wear appropriate formal or casual attire suitable for the event.

Prohibited Items

  • Carrying banned substances or any item that poses a safety risk is strictly prohibited.

Responsibility for Personal Belongings

  • Participants are responsible for their own belongings. Organizers will not be liable for any loss or damage.

Venue Regulations

  • Participants must respect the venue rules, maintain cleanliness, and avoid damage to property.
  • Any damage caused will be the responsibility of the team.

Team Identification

  • All participants must wear their team ID badges at all times during the event.

Fair Play

  • Any team found cheating, tampering with event processes, or violating rules will face immediate disqualification.

Organizer’s Authority

  • The organizers’ decision will be final and binding on all matters related to the event. No disputes will be entertained.

Web Wizards

W3B DESIXN

“Take on the ultimate web design showdown and stand out from the crowd! Craft original, functional, and stunning websites and prove you’ve got what it takes to dominate the web!”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • The participants will be given a theme based on which they have to design a dynamic website.
  • Website should be designed using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • For backend, only PHP and MySQL are allowed.
  • Premade templates are not allowed.
  • Website will be judged based on originality, design, functionality, and usability.

FigMatrix

"Step into the Figma Design Arena: Conceptualise, Craft, and Prototype Stunning Web App Interfaces!"

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hours

Rules
  • Participants will be given a theme at the start of the event.
  • The web app interface must be designed entirely in Figma.
  • Teams must create multiple screens and link them into a clickable prototype.
  • Use of pre-made templates is not allowed; all work must be original.
  • Designs must be completed and submitted within the given time limit.
  • Judgment will be based on creativity, usability, consistency, and overall visual appeal.

BugIT

“Not All Bugs Hide Well - Find them, fix them, and optimize like a true web detective.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 2 hours

Rules
  • The participants will be provided with a static website created with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.
  • The website does not have server side programming and database connection.
  • The participants have to identify the errors, document the errors in the template provided, fix the errors and optimize the website for performance.

Code Crusaders

CodeLite

“CodeLite is a challenge focused on writing efficient solutions with minimal instructions.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • Participants should have the knowledge of Python programming language.
  • The primary winning criterion is the shortest correct code.
  • Teams are not permitted to use external resources or internet access.
  • Libraries may be used only if explicitly stated.
  • All submitted codes must be valid, executable, and free of errors.
  • Plagiarism or copying code from other teams will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Any violation of the rules may result in disqualification.
  • Code completion time will be taken into consideration in case of a clash.

Code Hunt

“Code Hunt is the event where cryptography meets C programming—team up, decrypt the message, and bring the code to life.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 2 hours

Rules
  • Only college-provided systems may be used; personal devices are prohibited.
  • Participants must be familiar with Caesar Cipher techniques and C programming.
  • Teams consist of two members assigned as Decryptor and Coder.
  • Round 1: The Decryptor decodes the message while the Coder implements it in C.
  • Roles cannot be changed during a round.
  • Round 2: Roles are reversed.
  • Communication will be permitted only to share the decoded message.
  • Use of external resources or online assistance is strictly forbidden unless authorized.
  • Any rule violation may result in disqualification.
  • The team completing all tasks correctly in the shortest time will be declared the winner.

Bug Squashers

“Ready to squash some bugs? Step into the arena and show your mastery of C!”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • Participants should know C programming language.
  • Questions will consist of code snippets with a bug in the code.
  • Participants need to find the bug and write the correct code on the answer sheet.
  • Participants will not be allowed to use any IDE.
  • Participants are not allowed to carry any smart devices.

InnoQuest

Geek-O-Pedia

“A thrilling showdown of knowledge awaits—will your mind crack under pressure or lead you to triumph?”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • The quiz will be based on the following themes: Technology, General Knowledge, Logical Reasoning, Sports and Movies.
  • Only the top 5 qualifying teams from the elimination rounds (if held) will advance to the on-stage round.
  • The rules for each round will be announced at the beginning of the respective round.
  • The decision of the quiz-master and the organisers will be final and binding.

Pitch Perfect

“Where innovative ideas take center stage and creativity meets opportunity! Are you ready to pitch your vision?”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • Participants should pitch an innovative tech-based idea or concept for a product or service, showcasing its value, potential impact, and the transformative difference it can make.
  • The idea/concept could be presented by the participants with the aid of a model, demo video, or PowerPoint presentation.
  • Time duration : 10 minutes per team

Rebuttal Rebels

“Speak with speed, argue with logic, and defend with confidence! Are you ready to argue, defend, and dominate?”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 2 hour

Rules
  • Rebuttal Rebels is a debate competition centered around the latest technology topics.
  • Participants should come prepared with up-to-date knowledge of the trends and developments in the IT world.
  • Topics will be given on the spot, and participants must think quickly and present their arguments clearly.
  • Participants are expected to follow the debate rules, including time limits for speaking, respectful engagement with others, and staying on-topic.

Media Crafters

Creatix

“Your ideas, your art, your stage—make a poster that speaks volumes.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • Participants are required to create a poster on a given theme using Canva.
  • All posters must be created from a blank canvas.
  • Participants are not allowed to use any pre-made templates, themes, or design frameworks available on Canva.
  • Participants may use Canva’s basic elements (shapes, icons, fonts, colors) but must ensure original composition.
  • All textual content must be original and relevant to the theme.

SyncIT

“From concept to cut, craft a video that resonates and captivates.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 1½ hour

Rules
  • Participants will be provided with muted video clips and audio files.
  • The task is to synchronize ANY ONE of the video clips with one or more audio files to create a meaningful short movie using -Shortcut video editor / Openshot Video Editor.
  • Participants have to carry their own headphones (wired).

BlenderNate

“Bring your ideas off the screen—model, render, and amaze.”

Participants: 2   |   Duration: 2 hours

Rules
  • Participants must create a 3D model or scene based on the provided theme.
  • The theme will be communicated to participants at the beginning of the competition.
  • Software to be used : Blender
  • The participants are not allowed to use their own laptop, but must use the PC provided by the college.
  • Use of pre-built models, add-ons, or external assets is not allowed.

Swing of Fortune

Squad Reveal

“Kick-start the excitement by introducing your squad in a fun and creative way! Squad Reveal is a video-based icebreaker where teams express their identity, bond, and vibe through a short and engaging clip.”

Rules
  • This is a video-only introduction round. Teams are not permitted to introduce themselves live on stage.
  • Each team must prepare one icebreaker video.
  • One designated member from the team of 10 is responsible for uploading the video.
  • The video must be uploaded to the official Google Drive link shared by the organizers within the given deadline.
  • The maximum duration of the video should not exceed 5 minutes.
  • Participation in this icebreaker round is mandatory. Teams that do not submit a video will lose points that count toward the overall event score.

The Reel Run

“Bring NOESIS to life through your lens! The Reel Run challenges teams to capture the fun, energy, and unforgettable moments of the fest and present them as a creative Instagram reel.”

Rules
  • Each team is permitted to submit only one reel.
  • The reel must include clips captured during NOESIS event and within the college campus.
  • The duration of the reel must not exceed 2 minutes.
  • Teams must upload the reel on Instagram using the official event hashtag #NoesisOnCampus and tag the official event handle @noesis2k25_26.
  • The reel must be uploaded on or before 11:30 PM, 23rd January 2026.
  • The content must be appropriate, respectful, and aligned with the spirit of the fest.Any violation may lead to disqualification.
  • Participation is mandatory.Teams that fail to submit a reel will lose points, which will affect their overall standing.

Gamers Groove

FIFA 24

Participants: 1   Duration:1.5 hrs

Rules
  • Platform: PS5 (Controllers will be provided).
  • Only one participant per team is allowed.
  • Match Format: 4-minute halves with 1-minute team management per half.
  • Default camera settings must be used.
  • Teams from the 'Rest of the World' category are prohibited.
  • Tournament format will be decided based on the number of teams entered.
  • Swearing or sledging is not allowed.First a warning will be given, followed by disqualification if repeated.
  • Technical issues will be fairly resolved by the coordinator. First a warning will be given, followed by disqualification if repeated.
  • Extra time is allowed only in case of a draw in Semi-Finals and Finals. If still tied, matches will go directly to penalties.
  • Two minutes of pre-game time will be provided for team instructions and changing settings.
  • The decision of the organizers will be final and binding.
  • Any damage to the controller must be paid for by the participant responsible.

Valorant

Participants: Team (Max 5 players per match)

Rules
  • Participants are required to bring and use their own laptops for the gaming event.
  • Valorant will be played in team format. Teams will be formed by the organizers based on the participation.
  • Game Mode for early rounds will be informed during the event.
  • The game frame rate will be capped at 60 FPS to ensure equal performance conditions.
  • All matches will be played on systems with a 60 Hz refresh rate for fairness.
  • Matches will be hosted on the Mumbai server or the nearest mutually agreed server to minimize latency.
  • Each team is allowed one tactical pause per match lasting up to two minutes.
